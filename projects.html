Project Breakdown

Here you can find a deeper explanation of what I developed in each project, how I approached the work, and the most relevant technical decisions.

Cloud Walker

What I built: A cozy 2D mobile game as my Bachelor's Final Project, focused on exploration and collecting unique birds in procedurally generated islands.

How I built it: I implemented a procedural generation pipeline using Wave Function Collapse (WFC), balancing generation rules to keep each island valid and fun. I also developed gameplay systems for progression, interaction and collection tracking.

  • Gameplay systems in Unity with C#.
  • Procedural content generation with WFC constraints.
  • Optimization and iteration for mobile performance.

Erebus-Nyx

What I built: A survival psychological horror game experience on an abandoned planet.

How I built it: Worked with Unreal Engine 5 and Blueprints to implement mechanics that support tension and player uncertainty, while integrating level logic and event-driven interactions.

  • Gameplay prototyping and tuning in UE5.
  • Blueprint systems for interactions and sequences.
  • Collaborative development workflow with Git.

Dude L

What I built: A 2D platformer/puzzle adventure where a notebook doodle comes to life.

How I built it: I coordinated gameplay tasks and helped define level flow and player progression. The core implementation focused on movement feel, puzzle readability and challenge pacing.

  • Unity/C# gameplay and puzzle implementation.
  • Team coordination and feature planning.
  • Iteration based on testing feedback.

Rocket Land

What I built: A 2D precision platformer where players pilot a ship through hazardous levels and controlled landings.

How I built it: As a solo project, I designed and developed all mechanics from scratch in Unity, with a strong focus on responsive controls, obstacle readability and level learning curve.

  • Solo end-to-end development.
  • Physics tuning for precision movement.
  • Level design focused on timing and control.

Metal y Plomo

What I built: A 2D platformer with puzzle and combat components, including grappling-based traversal.

How I built it: In a team setting, I contributed to gameplay logic, puzzle interactions and level progression. The objective was to create fast traversal while keeping puzzle complexity understandable.

  • Gameplay and puzzle collaboration in Construct.
  • Iteration on mobility mechanics and challenge.
  • Cross-discipline teamwork.
